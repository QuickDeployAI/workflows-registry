## Abstract

A short (~200 word) description of the technical issue being addressed.

## Motivation

Why the existing registry/contract/process is inadequate. SEPs without
sufficient motivation may be rejected outright.

## Specification

The technical specification: syntax and semantics, detailed enough for
competing, interoperable implementations. Use normative MUST/SHOULD language
so conformance tests can trace to individual requirements.

## Rationale

Why these design decisions were made; alternatives considered and rejected;
evidence of consensus; objections raised during discussion and how they were
addressed.

## Backward Compatibility

Incompatibilities introduced (if any), their severity, and how they are
handled. State explicitly when a proposal is purely additive.

## Reference Implementation

Where the implementation will live and how it will be verified. Must be
complete before the SEP reaches `final`; a runnable prototype must exist
before `accepted`.

## Security Implications

Explicit discussion of security concerns and how the design addresses them.

## Open Questions

Anything deliberately left unresolved for review discussion. (Optional)

## References

Related SEPs, documents, and prior art. (Optional)
